# Accuracy Disclaimer

---

## Formal Disclaimer

The material quantity estimates generated by Metraj are provided for **planning and budgeting purposes only**. They are not a substitute for professional quantity surveying, structural engineering analysis, or detailed construction planning.

---

## Scope of Estimates

Metraj generates material quantity estimates based on:

1. **Building element data** extracted from IFC (Industry Foundation Classes) files
2. **Automated quantity calculations** derived from element dimensions and properties
3. **AI-assisted material mapping** using the Anthropic Claude API to determine construction materials
4. **Industry-average waste factors** applied to net quantities

The estimates cover permanent construction materials identifiable from the building model. They do not include temporary works, MEP systems (unless modeled), site-specific requirements, or construction method variations.

---

## Limitations and Exclusions

Users should understand that:

- **Model dependency:** All quantities are derived from the IFC building model as provided. Errors, omissions, or simplifications in the model will be directly reflected in the estimates. If elements are not modeled, they will not appear in the BOQ.

- **Waste factors are generalizations:** The waste factors applied (ranging from 2% for glass to 15% for timber formwork) are industry averages. Actual waste depends on construction method, labor skill, site conditions, material quality, weather, and project scale. These factors should be reviewed and adjusted for specific project conditions.

- **Reinforcement is approximate:** Steel reinforcement quantities are estimated using standard kg/m3 ratios (60-180 kg/m3 depending on element type). Actual reinforcement requirements depend on structural analysis including load calculations, span dimensions, seismic design, and code requirements. These estimates should not be used for rebar procurement without verification against structural drawings.

- **AI-generated content may contain errors:** Three pipeline stages use artificial intelligence (Claude by Anthropic) for element classification, material determination, and quality review. AI systems can produce incorrect outputs including misclassification of elements, omission of necessary materials, incorrect material specifications, or inaccurate quantity multipliers.

- **No structural analysis:** Metraj does not perform structural analysis. Material specifications (concrete grades, steel grades) are based on common construction practice, not on engineering calculations specific to the building.

- **No code compliance verification:** The estimates do not verify compliance with local building codes, fire safety regulations, energy efficiency requirements, or accessibility standards.

- **No site-specific considerations:** The system does not account for soil conditions, seismic zones, climate factors, site access constraints, or other location-specific requirements that affect material selection and quantities.

## User Responsibilities

By using Metraj, you accept that:

1. **Professional review is required.** All estimates should be reviewed and verified by a qualified quantity surveyor or construction professional before being used for procurement, tendering, contract preparation, or construction planning.

2. **You are responsible for verification.** The user bears full responsibility for verifying the accuracy and completeness of the generated estimates before relying on them for any purpose.

3. **Adjustments may be needed.** The estimates should be considered a starting point that requires adjustment based on project-specific conditions, local market factors, and professional judgment.

4. **No warranty of accuracy.** The estimates are provided "as-is" without warranty of accuracy, completeness, or fitness for any particular purpose.

5. **Data is sent to external services.** Element-level building data (types, names, quantities, materials -- not full geometry) is sent to the Anthropic Claude API during processing. You are responsible for ensuring this is acceptable for your project and organization.

## Recommended Use Cases

Metraj estimates are most appropriate for:

- **Early-stage budgeting** -- rough cost estimates during feasibility and concept design
- **Design comparison** -- comparing material quantities between design alternatives
- **Quantity verification** -- cross-checking manual quantity takeoffs
- **Teaching and training** -- understanding the relationship between building elements and materials
- **Preliminary procurement planning** -- initial material lists for supplier discussions

Metraj estimates should **not** be used as the sole basis for:

- Final procurement orders
- Contractual quantities in construction contracts
- Tender documents without independent verification
- Insurance valuations
- Regulatory compliance documentation
